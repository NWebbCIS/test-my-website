Training German Shepherds

Getting Started

When deciding if a German Shepherd is the right addition for your family, it is important to know that they are a very intelligent and loyal breed. Due to this, they have a high trainability level but require more mental stimulation than the average dog breed. Also, due to their history as working dogs, German Shepherds also have a high energy level and require regular exercise.

Potty Training

When potty training a German Shepherd, it is important to be proactive instead of reactive. Paying attention to your puppy's potty cues, such as sniffing around, circling, and whining. Setting a routine for your puppy to go outside to go potty and offering positive reinforcement when they do is essential to successful potty training.

Leash Training

  1. Introduce your puppy to the collar or harness they will be wearing when going for a walk.
  2. While inside, teach your puppy a sound cue. When they look at you, reward their attention with a treat.
  3. While inside, after giving the sound cue, while your puppy is approaching you while wearing the leash or harness, back up a few paces. When they follow you, reward them with a treat.
  4. Walk your puppy outside where more distractions are present. If your puppy is about to be distracted, use the sound cue to regain their attention and reward that attention with a treat.

Socialization

Early socialization is an important part of a puppy's education, especially for a larger, intelligent breed like a German Shepherd. Socialization helps prevent your puppy from growing up fearful and aggressive.

Exposing your puppy to new sights, sounds, and smells in a calm environment while using positive reinforcement is a good first step to socializing your puppy. Next, start introducing your puppy to one or two new people at a time. Once your puppy grows comfortable around strangers in a calm setting, you can try to expand their comfort level by taking them out into public.
